What is bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a digital currency (also called a cryptocurrency)
that was created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group using the name Satoshi Nakamoto.

Decentralized: It isn’t controlled by any government, bank, or company. Instead, it runs on a peer-to-peer network.
Blockchain technology: All transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain, which is maintained by computers (nodes) around the world.
Limited supply: There will only ever be 21 million bitcoins, making it scarce like gold.
Mining: New bitcoins are created through a process called mining, where powerful computers solve complex problems to validate transactions.
Digital ownership: People store and transfer bitcoin using digital wallets, which are secured with cryptographic keys.
Use cases: It can be used for payments, remittances, or as a form of investment (often compared to “digital gold”).
